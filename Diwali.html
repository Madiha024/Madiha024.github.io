What is Diwali?

A Hindu holiday called Diwali honors the victory of right over wrong, the benefits of victory, freedom, and enlightenment. Diyas, candles, and lanterns are used by Hindus, Jains, and Sikhs to light their residences, temples, and workplaces. Fireworks, rangoli floor decorations, and jhalar decorations are all used to decorate the home for Diwali. The celebration of Diwali honors Rama and Sita's triumphal journey from the wilderness to Ayodhya, where Rama is anointed king, in the Ramayana. The first day of the five-day festival of lights, Dhanteras, is when wealth is worshipped. People apply ubtan and take a holy bath on Narak Chaturdashi. Goddess Lakshmi is reputed to visit houses and bestow good fortune on people during Lakshmi Puja. On the occasion of Padva Puja, Lord Krishna raised the Govardhan Mountain to subjugate Indra. On Bhai Dooj, sisters host their brothers with a magnificent meal and hold a 'tilak' ceremony in order to ask for a long and prosperous life for their family.
