What is Constitutional Day and why is it celebrated?

The United States celebrates Constitution Day every time to flash back when the U.S. Constitution was ratified on September 17, 1787. The day honors the nation's founding constitution, which set up the structure for the civil government and assured people's rights and liberties. On Constitution Day, people in the United States have the chance to break and examine the Constitution's significance throughout American history and its connection now.
