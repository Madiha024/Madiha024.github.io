What is Mardi Gras and how is it celebrated?

Mardi Gras is an annual carnival festival that takes place in numerous locations throughout the world, most notably in New Orleans, Louisiana. It is held on the day before Ash Wednesday, which ushers in the Christian season of Lent. The tradition of feasting and celebrating before the Lenten season was widespread throughout medieval Europe, and was brought to Louisiana by French settlers in the 17th and 18th centuries. Mardi Gras festivities in New Orleans are renowned for their grandiose parades, vibrant costumes, and exciting street parties. The King Cake, a delectable cake with a tiny plastic baby baked inside, is a tradition that is believed to be lucky for the upcoming year. People also dress up in elaborate costumes and masks to exhibit their creativity and have fun before the more solemn season of Lent begins.
