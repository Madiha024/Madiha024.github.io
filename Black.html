What is Black History Month and why is it celebrated?

Black History Month is an annual event that honors and recognizes the achievements made by African Americans to the history and culture of the United States, Canada, and a number of other nations. In the US, Canada, and the UK, it is observed in the month of February. In the UK, it is observed in the month of October.

To raise awareness of the battles and difficulties endured by African Americans throughout their history in the United States is one of the main objectives of Black History Month. From slavery and Jim Crow segregation to the civil rights movement and ongoing struggles for racial justice, black Americans have faced significant obstacles and discrimination throughout their history.

The appreciation of the numerous achievements made by African Americans to the national culture and society is another significant component of Black History Month. Black People have significantly contributed to almost every aspect of American culture, from music and art to literature and science.
