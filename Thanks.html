What is Thanksgiving and why is it celebrated?

Thanksgiving is a holiday that is observed in several nations, including the US, Canada, and Liberia. It is usually observed on the fourth Thursday of November in the US and the second Monday of October in Canada. This day is dedicated to giving thanks for the harvest and the previous year's gifts.

Thanksgiving is an important holiday that is observed in the United States by families and friends coming together to enjoy a meal. Thanksgiving dinner customarily consists of turkey, stuffing, cranberry sauce, mashed potatoes, and pumpkin pie. Many families follow their own distinctive customs and recipes.
