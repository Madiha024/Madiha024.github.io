What is Thanksgiving and why is it celebrated?

Thanksgiving is a holiday celebrated in several countries, including the United States, Canada, and Liberia, typically on the fourth Thursday of November in the US, and the second Monday of October in Canada. It is a day of giving thanks for the blessings of the harvest and of the preceding year.

In the United States, Thanksgiving is a significant holiday celebrated by families and friends gathering together to share a meal. The traditional Thanksgiving meal typically includes turkey, stuffing, cranberry sauce, mashed potatoes, and pumpkin pie. Many families have their own unique recipes and traditions that they follow.
