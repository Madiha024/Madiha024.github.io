What is Glastonbury and how is it celebrated?

A small town in Somerset, England called Glastonbury has gained worldwide recognition for its yearly music event. One of the biggest and most well-known music events in the world, the festival, formally known as the Glastonbury Festival of Contemporary Performing Arts, draws hundreds of thousands of visitors each year.

The town of Glastonbury is thought to have been a place of religious importance for thousands of years, so its history can be traced back to prehistoric times. It is home to the famous Glastonbury Tor, a hill that rises dramatically from the surrounding landscape, and is rumored to be the location of the fabled Isle of Avalon, where King Arthur was buried.

This small village has become well-known thanks to the Glastonbury Festival. A group of friends started the event in 1970 by hosting a music festival on a farm close to the town. The festival began as a single-day affair but rapidly gained popularity and drew some of the biggest musical stars.
