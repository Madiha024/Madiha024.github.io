What is Mother's Day and why is it celebrated?

Mother's Day is a unique holiday observed worldwide to recognize and respect mothers' love, care, and sacrifices. The day is marked on various days in different nations, but throughout most of the world, it is typically observed on the second Sunday in May.

People generally honor their moms on Mother's Day by giving them cards, flowers, gifts, or other kinds of expressions of gratitude. Families frequently gather together for time spent at home, on important occasions, or even just to go on an outing.
