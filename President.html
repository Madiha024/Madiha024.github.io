What is Presidents' Day and why is it celebrated?

President's Day is a federal holiday celebrated in the United States of America on the third Monday of February every year. It is a day to honor and remember the accomplishments of past U.S. presidents, especially George Washington, the country's first president.

On President's Day, many schools and businesses are closed, and there are often parades, speeches, and other celebrations to mark the occasion.
