What is Ganesh Chaturthi?

Ganesha is a popular God. He has an Elephant's head. For this festival, in western India, people make clay images of Ganesha. They place them in their home shrines. At morning and evening prayers, they pray to Ganesha. This festival is also known as Vinayak Chaturthi or Ganeshotsav and commemorates the borth of Ganesha. The festival ends on tenth day after start, when the idol is carried in a public procession with music and group chanting, then immersed in a nearby body of water such as a river or sea, this is called visarjan.
