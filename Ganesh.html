What is Ganesh Chaturthi?

Ganesha is a famous deity. His head resembles an elephant's. In recognition of this occasion, people in western India sculpt clay Ganesha idols. In their individual shrines, they place them. At their morning and evening prayers, they make supplications to Ganesha. This holiday, also known as Vinayak Chaturthi or Ganeshotsav, marks Ganesha's conception. The idol is paraded through the streets in a public procession on the tenth day following the festival's start while music is playing and people are singing; this is called visarjan. After then, the idol is immersed in a nearby body of water, either a river or the sea.
