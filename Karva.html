What is Karva Chauth?

A celebration called Karva Chauth is observed on the fourth day following the full moon known as Purnima in the month of Ashwin. Karva Chauth, like many Hindu festivals, is based on the lunisolar calendar, which takes into consideration all astronomical locations, particularly the positions of the moon, which is used as a marker to determine crucial dates. Married women and single women alike fast from sunrise until moonrise on this day in particular to ensure the security and longevity of their husbands.

Women start getting ready for Karva Chauth a few days beforehand by purchasing jewelry, adornments (shringar), and puja materials like the Karva lamps, matthi, Mehandi, and the adorned puja thali(plate). Local traders showcase their Karva Chauth-related merchandise, giving the bazaars(shops) a festive appearance.
