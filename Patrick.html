What is St. Patricks Day and how is it celebrated?

Every year on March 17, people across the world mark St. Patrick's Day, also known as the Feast of Saint Patrick, in honor of the patron saint of Ireland. It has been observed as a religious vacation in Ireland since the 17th century, and is celebrated not only in Ireland but also in numerous other countries across the world. One of the most popular traditions is the cortege, which was held in New York City in 1762 by Irish dog faces serving in the British army. Another well-known custom is eating and drinking traditional Irish food, such as soda bread, corned beef and cabbage, and Guinness beer. Green beer is served in some pubs and taverns to mark the anniversary, and the river in Chicago is dyed green.
