What is April Fool's Day and why is it celebrated?

On April 1st each year, many nations around the globe celebrate April Fool's Day. It's a day set aside for practical jokes, scams, and other forms of social trickery. It is a fun festival that fosters camaraderie among its participants through jokes and laughter.

It is a popular holiday celebrated in many countries, including the United States, Canada, Australia, and the United Kingdom.

People frequently pull harmless pranks on their peers and family members on April 1st. A lot of businesses and groups join in the fun as well, frequently using April 1st as a chance to advertise their brand and generate buzz on social media. The most well-known examples include Google's yearly April Fool's Day jokes, which have included the "Google Nose" search engine, which purported to let users look up smells, and the "Google Maps" treasure hunt, which required users to locate a concealed treasure.
