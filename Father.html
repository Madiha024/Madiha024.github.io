What is Father's Day and why is it celebrated?

Father's Day is a unique holiday honoring the importance of dads in our lives. The purpose of the holiday is to thank fathers for their affection and support of their families as well as to recognize the contributions and sacrifices they make. Father's Day is observed on various days around the world, but in many, including the United States, it is customarily observed on the third Sunday in June. It's a time to thank them for their affection and support and to acknowledge the sacrifices and effort they make on behalf of their families. Father's Day is a chance to express gratitude and to fortify the bond between fathers, whether through gifts, activities, or just quality time spent together.
