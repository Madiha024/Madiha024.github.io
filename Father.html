What is Father's Day and why is it celebrated?

Father's Day is an annual celebration that recognizes the significance of fathers in our lives. The holiday's motivation is to express gratitude to fathers for their love and support for their families as well as to honor the efforts and sacrifices they make. Around the world, Father's Day is celebrated on a variety of dates, but in many, including the United States, it is typically celebrated on the third Sunday in June. It's a chance to express gratitude for their kindness and support as well as to recognize the efforts and sacrifices they make on behalf of their family. Whether through presents, activities, or just quality time spent together, Father's Day is an opportunity to show appreciation and strengthen the link between fathers.
