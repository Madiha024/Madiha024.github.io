What is Eid-ul-Fitr?

Muslims all across the world celebrate Eid-ul-Fitr because it signifies the conclusion of Ramadan's one-month long dawn to dusk fast. It usually starts the day after the new crescent moon is seen. Muslims maintain their fast for one extra day if necessary in order to see the moon. During the first three days of Shawwal, it is observed. (10th month of Islamic calendar after the month of Ramadan). Takbeer: Allaahu akbar, Allaahu akbar, laa ilaaha ill-Allaah, Allaahu akbar, Allaahu akbar, wa Lillaahi'l-hamd (Allaah is Most Great, Allaah is Most Great, there is no god except Allaah, Allaah is Most Great, Allaah is Most Great, and all praise be to Allaah) is recited before Eid prayer.

Etiquettes of Eid:

1. Taking a shower (ghusl) before leaving for the prayer

2. Eating dates before before leaving for the prayer

3. Takbeer on the day of Eid

4. Offering Congratulations

5. Adorning oneself in celebration of Eid

6. Taking a different path to and from the prayer than usual.
