What is Valentine's Day and why is it celebrated?

The yearly holiday of Valentine's Day, also known as Saint Valentine's Day, is observed on February 14. It is a day to show love and affection to close friends, family members, and other loved ones. Valentine, a Catholic martyr who lived in the third century, is honored on this day. Today, people all across the world commemorate the event thanks to its centuries-long evolution.

Valentine's Day is traditionally associated with romantic love, with couples exchanging cards, flowers, and gifts to express their love for each other. It is a day to celebrate the love and commitment shared between two people. Red roses, heart-shaped chocolates, and jewelry are popular gifts exchanged on Valentine's Day.

However, the holiday is not just for couples in romantic relationships. It is also a day to celebrate friendships and familial love. Many people exchange cards and gifts with their friends and family members to show appreciation for their love and support.
